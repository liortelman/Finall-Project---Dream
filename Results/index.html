Key findings
🏆
All models beat humans
With semantic labels, every model exceeded the 67% human baseline — the top model (ChatGPT) reached 98%.
🏷️
Labels matter enormously
Adding axis labels boosted open-source models by up to 40 percentage points, while commercial models were already strong without labels.
📉
PC1 is universally hard
The narrative vs. fragmented axis (PC1) was the hardest for every model and humans alike — even top models scored only 70–90% there.
⚡
Small models fail
LLAMA 3.1 8B scored only 17% in both conditions — below chance — suggesting a minimum model size is needed for this task.
🌍
Language parity
Hebrew and English human participants performed equally (67% vs 66%), confirming cross-linguistic validity of the PCA structure.
✅
PCA validated
High AI accuracy with labels confirms that the PCA axes capture meaningful, human-interpretable semantic dimensions of dreams.
